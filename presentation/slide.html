Project Objectives

Background

In a competitive e-commerce landscape, understanding and retaining customers is critical for sustainable growth. This project aims to transform raw transactional data into actionable business intelligence.

Core Questions

  • ➜ Who are our customers? By segmenting users into distinct value groups.
  • ➜ Who might we lose? By proactively identifying customers at high risk of churning.

Part 3

Descriptive Analysis

03

Understanding RFM Distributions

Interpretation

These histograms show the distribution of RFM values across all customers.

  • Recency (R): A large number of customers have high Recency values (haven't purchased in a long time), an early indicator of our churn problem.
  • Frequency (F) & Monetary (M): Both distributions are heavily right-skewed. This classic retail pattern shows that most customers purchase infrequently, while a small group of power users are responsible for high spending.
03

Finding: Customer Segment Distribution

Interpretation

This chart displays the final customer segments, providing a clear snapshot of our customer base's health.

  • Key Challenge: The "At-Risk" segment is the largest, confirming that customer retention is the most critical issue to address.
  • Core Strength: The business is supported by a strong foundation of "Champions" and "Loyal Customers", who are the primary revenue drivers.

Part 4

Predictive Analysis

04

Finding: Predictive Model Performance

Interpretation

We trained a Logistic Regression model to predict churn. The confusion matrix shows its performance on unseen test data.

  • High Accuracy: The model achieves an overall accuracy of approximately 90%.
  • Excellent Recall: More importantly, the model has a Recall of 90% for the churn class. This means it successfully identifies 9 out of 10 customers who are actually going to churn, making it highly effective for targeted retention campaigns.
  • Business Value: This predictive power allows the business to move from being reactive to proactive, saving customers before they are lost.

Part 5

Recommendations & Conclusion

05

Actionable Recommendations

For Champions

Goal: Reward & Retain

  • Implement a VIP program with exclusive benefits.
  • Encourage them to become brand advocates.

For At-Risk Customers

Goal: Proactive Reactivation

  • Use the predictive model to target this group.
  • Launch "win-back" campaigns with personalized discounts.

For New Customers

Goal: Nurture & Convert

  • Create a welcome email series to build engagement.
  • Offer an incentive for their second purchase.
